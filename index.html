OUR MENU

Ampesie

Ampesie is a staple Ghanaian dish consisting of boiled starchy vegetables—typically yam, green plantain, cocoyam, or cassava—served with a rich stew or sauce

kenkey

Kenkey is a staple Ghanaian dish made from fermented white corn dough that is shaped into balls, wrapped in husks or leaves, and boiled

Tuo Zafi

a staple "swallow" dish from Northern Ghana. The name originates from the Hausa language: "Tuo" means stirring or paddling, and "Zaafi" means hot, literally translating to "very hot tuo"

GOB3

one of Ghana's most popular and beloved street foods. It is a nutritious, high-protein meal made primarily from boiled beans and gari (cassava flakes).
